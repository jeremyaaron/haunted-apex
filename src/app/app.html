Network Reference

Field Guide

The Roster
Build Dominion to 90 before week 8 closes.

Heat at 100, Loyalty at 0, or Resources below 0 ends the run. Action cards recalculate their displayed outcome and complication risk as targets and operatives change.

Running a week

Each week begins on the Command Board with a pool of Command points. Queue orders by choosing actions and paying the Command cost printed on each card. You may assign an operative or select a target where the action allows it; the card preview updates before you commit the order.

Queue at least one order, then Advance Week when your plan is ready. Queued orders resolve in sequence, followed by weekly fallout and a city event. Choose one event response to apply its consequences and begin the next week. Unspent Command does not carry forward.

Actions and risk

Risk is the percentage chance that an order suffers a complication. A complication worsens the resolved outcome; it is not the chance that the order produces nothing.

Gather Intel tests Subtlety, Small Jobs test Violence, and Bribe Official plus Expand Influence test Charm. Higher relevant skill and Loyalty reduce risk. Stress raises it, especially at 60 or higher. Some operatives also have action-specific advantages already included in the preview.

The roster, recruitment, and Stress

Every seed assembles three starting operatives and four visible hire candidates. Rarity changes how often an operative appears, not whether they are stronger. Role tags are quick strategic signals; dossiers provide the full stats, visible traits, liabilities, affinities, and recent assignment history.

Stable, Strained, Unstable, and Breaking Stress tiers add progressively more complication risk. Breaking operatives remain assignable, but using them invites severe personal fallout. Action previews show the visible traits and affinities that currently match, plus the projected Stress tier.

Recruitment is a normal queued order: select Recruit Operative on the Command Board, choose a candidate, pay its Resource and Command costs, then advance the week. The active roster can hold five operatives.

Districts, venues, and control

Control is your network foothold in a district, not its ownership rating. Higher Control reduces action risk at 40 and again at 70. Gather Intel, Small Jobs, and Expand Influence can raise it. Rival ownership is fixed in this release, so even high Control does not transfer a district or venue to you.

Local Heat makes future work in that district riskier when it rises above the district's base. It cools toward base each week. Venues modify only outcomes an action already affects: Wealth modifies Resources, while the other labels match their pressure meters.

Rivals and pressure

Targeting a rival or property they control raises that kingpin's Pressure. Pressure status advances through Watching (0-24), Interested (25-49), Provoked (50-74), and Retaliating (75-100). Higher Pressure also changes which city events are likely to surface.

At 40 Pressure, Knox can add Heat each week. Nyx can erode Loyalty each week when your Intel is below 20. Attack names the pressure meter each rival prefers to damage. Disposition and the remaining profile ratings are intelligence for future systems; they do not modify actions in this release.

The Black Ledger

The Ledger records leverage created by actions and event responses. Secrets are useful information, Debts are obligations that can return as pressure, and Favors are rare comeback tools. Consumed or resolved entries stay visible as history.

Targeted Gather Intel can discover Secrets when the target has matching hidden opportunities. Work the Ledger spends a Command point to use an active Secret, settle a Debt, or call a Favor. Event choices show their Ledger consequences before you choose.

Entanglements and contacts

Each run exposes three active outside Contacts. Use Manage Contact to cultivate trust, apply pressure, or request a specific service. Contacts do not use operative assignment; choose one Contact option from the Target dropdown and queue it like any other command.

Trust and Leverage are useful reserves. Volatility and Exposure are danger signals. Contact options can spend Resources, Intel, Trust, or Leverage, then change both city pressures and Contact metrics. Preview chips show those costs and effects before you commit.

Services can create Ledger entries, raise rival Pressure, or offer special help such as Quiet Treatment for a stressed operative. Unavailable Contact options stay visible when the relationship is not ready, but burned Contacts cannot be used.

Fronts and exposure

Fronts are owned operations that turn Resources into weekly infrastructure. Invest in Front can establish an opening or upgrade an owned Front. Each Front has a Level, location, role tags, weekly yield, and Exposure status.

Weekly yield can add pressures such as Resources, Loyalty, Intel, Dominion, Heat, Ruin, or district Control. Exposure also rises each week, so strong Fronts become more visible the longer they operate. Lay Low can target an owned Front to cool Exposure without assigning an operative.

Front events use the normal weekly event slot. Higher Exposure makes a Front more likely to be selected when an eligible Front event fires, and event choices preview their Front, Ledger, and rival consequences before you respond.

Weekly fallout

Every advance resolves queued orders, operative stress, network upkeep, local cooling, rival reprisals, and one city event. The Event Feed records these changes. Leave room for upkeep and retaliation instead of budgeting only around the values printed on queued orders.
